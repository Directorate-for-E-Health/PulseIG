Example: Example Instances

These are example instances that show what data produced and consumed by systems conforming with this implementation guide might look like.

PatientExample

Example of Patient
Pulse1Example

A Home Care Nurse measures a patient’s pulse with pulse oximetry and the result is 112 beats/minute. The Home Care Nurse are told to be aware of Early Warning Score (EWS) and looks up time series on pulse rate (several types).
Pulse2Example

A patient is examined by a doctor where the pulse is observed by auscultation. The pulse rate is 118 beats/minute. The doctor fears infection and follows advice on antibiotics (AB). The patient has a pc-allergy (core record/kjernejournal), why the AB choice
Pulse3Example

A Home Care Nurse measures the patient’s pulse using blood pressure cuff inflator and the result is 104 beats/minute. After a while, the Home Care Nurse measures the patient’s pulse again using blood pressure cuff inflator and now the result is slightly higher: 110 beats/minute. The Home Care Nurse decides to measure the patient one last time using blood pressure cuff inflator before sending the observation data into the electronic patient record and this time the result has increased again and the pulse rate measurement is 115 beats/minute.
Pulse4Example

Clinician: Specialist health service (specialhelsetjenesten)/specialist doctor). Final diagnosis: Sepsis unlikely (focus transfer - data sharing). The specialist will look on the doctor’s assessment and hypothesis (use case 2) and the different pulse measurement results (use case 1-3), when assessing the final diagnosis.
